Owner: Chief Information Security Officer


1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes guidelines for the retention and secure disposal of data to ensure compliance with legal requirements, support business operations, and minimize risk from retaining data longer than necessary.


2. SCOPE

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and third-party processors
- All data created, received, or maintained by TechCorp
- All formats: electronic, paper, and other media
- All locations: on-premises, cloud, and remote storage


3. DEFINITIONS

3.1 Personal Data: Information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

3.2 Sensitive Data: Special category data including health information, financial records, government identifiers, and biometric data.

3.3 Retention Period: The length of time data must be kept before disposal.

3.4 Legal Hold: A directive to preserve data relevant to pending or anticipated litigation.

3.5 Data Disposal: The secure destruction or anonymization of data at end of retention.

5. RETENTION REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Minimum Retention
---------------------
Data must be retained for the minimum period required by:
a) Applicable laws and regulations
b) Contractual obligations
c) Business operational needs
d) This policy's retention schedule

5.2 Maximum Retention
---------------------
Data should NOT be retained beyond the retention period unless:
a) Subject to legal hold
b) Required for ongoing legal proceedings
c) Explicitly approved by Data Governance Committee

5.3 Legal Holds
---------------
When litigation is pending or anticipated:
1. Legal Department issues legal hold notice
2. Affected data custodians acknowledge receipt
3. Relevant data is preserved regardless of retention schedule
4. Normal disposal suspended for hold-affected data
5. Hold released only by Legal Department authorization


6. DATA DISPOSAL PROCEDURES

6.1 Electronic Data Disposal
----------------------------
Standard data:
- Secure deletion using approved tools (minimum 3-pass overwrite)
- Cryptographic erasure for encrypted storage

Sensitive/confidential data:
- NIST SP 800-88 compliant sanitization
- Physical destruction for storage media when decommissioned
- Certificate of destruction required

Cloud data:
- Follow cloud provider's secure deletion procedures
- Verify deletion across all replicas and backups
- Obtain confirmation from provider

6.2 Paper Document Disposal
---------------------------
Standard documents:
- Cross-cut shredding (minimum DIN Level P-4)

Confidential documents:
- Micro-cut shredding (DIN Level P-5 or higher)
- Use approved shredding vendor for bulk disposal
- Obtain certificate of destruction

6.3 Hardware Disposal
---------------------
Before disposal or transfer of any hardware:
1. Remove all data using approved sanitization methods
2. Verify sanitization with IT Security
3. Obtain sign-off from asset owner
4. Document disposal in asset management system


7. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

7.1 Data Governance Committee
-----------------------------
- Approve retention schedule changes
- Grant exceptions to retention periods
- Review policy annually

7.2 Department Heads
--------------------
- Ensure compliance within their departments
- Identify data requiring special retention
- Report retention issues to Data Governance

7.3 IT Department
-----------------
- Implement technical controls for retention
- Provide secure disposal tools and procedures
- Maintain disposal documentation

7.4 Legal Department
--------------------
- Issue and manage legal holds
- Advise on regulatory retention requirements
- Coordinate with external legal counsel

7.5 All Employees
-----------------
- Follow retention schedule for their data
- Report suspected retention violations
- Comply with legal hold notices


8. EXCEPTIONS

Exceptions to this policy require:
1. Written request to Data Governance Committee
2. Business justification
3. Risk assessment
4. Legal review
5. Documented approval
6. Annual review of exception status


9. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

9.1 Monitoring
--------------
- Regular audits of retention compliance
- Automated monitoring of storage systems
- Spot checks of disposal procedures

9.2 Violations
--------------
Violations may result in:
- Corrective action and retraining
- Disciplinary measures up to termination
- Legal liability for negligent disposal

9.3 Reporting
-------------
Report retention concerns to:
- Email: data-governance@techcorp.com
- Anonymous hotline: 1-800-ETHICS
